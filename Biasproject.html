CinemAll App

Role UX Designer
Team Ruturaj, Christa, Danielle
Type School Group Project

This projects aim at improving the in-theater movie experience for visually impaired moviegoers who currently need theater-provided descriptive video devices to enjoy a movie. I acted as a UX Designer for 3 months, collborating on semi-structured interviews and interface design.

THE CHALLENGE

Movie watch is not an universal experience

We surprisely discovered that many movies come with audio description tracks according to the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (ADA). Through our pilot research, we identified a neglected, yet attention-needed user segments, the visually impaired movielovers for theaters like AMC, Regal, Cinefest, Landmark, and others. To be more specific, we define our target users range in age from 18 and up, have the normal hearing function, span all genders, have anxieties about going to a movie theater, and may or may not bring a companion with them.

The Movie Journeys

Through user interviews and participating in the journey, we discovered that users have to be well prepared before getting the theater knowing where have and which associate they should contact to borrow the descriptive headset.

Those "Losing-Users" Moments

1) Before the movie, requiring users to interact with less professional associates, will caused them feeling not valued and not prepared well enough. Also, extra personal information leakage cause some users feeling suspected.

2) In the movie, users have to deal with the headset error by approaching to the front desk,which disrupts users immersive experience and angry for missing important scenes.

BRAINSTORMING

One Kiosk, two Apps

Concept 1: Smart Kiosks

This solution will aim to cut out interaction with theater associates, who are often times undertrained and cause unnecessary frustrations for visually impaired moviegoers. Additionally, we envision the kiosks to be a universal solution and a one-stop shop for all moviegoers to purchase or pickup tickets and retrieve assistive devices.

DESIGN TRADEOFF

Download Can Harm Theater's Interest

We assume that the pupularity of our intended app has great influence with the ability to provide the lateset descriptive audio track. However, the movie producers' right is equally important for a success launch of this product. We have to prevent users take advantage of this app to leak and disseminate new movie content illegally.

Style Guide Design

Design for the Visually Impaired

To design for the visually impaired users, none of us have previous experience. We thus referenced some accessible design guidelines to learn how to design for the visually impaired users. We tried to maintain a 5.04 constrast ratio which pass WebAIM standard 4.5:1 as well as maintaining a simple and single column layout; We also maintained a black theme considering the dark showroom environment.

Reflections

What I learned from the Projects

Let users talk. At the very begining, we were trapped with our own experience and had a hard time identifying a valueable key points. However, when we went out and talked with the real users, their stories inspired us a lot. I really appreciate that when we were doing the pilot interview, Christa asked the participants, "Besides eating, is there other scenarios that you were experiencing?" And when the visually impaired participants tell her movie stories, we were so excited and inspired. Talk with users allow us to break the border of designers' own life and gain empathy towards our users.

Considering the context when designing tasks. This App serves for a whole process from purchasing movie tickets to walk out the theater. Different contexts have high influence on how each stakeholder behavea. For example, the manager at the Regal Cinema told us, they wouldn't provide service prior the arrival of the users. However, the visually impaired users would love to be prepared before their arrival. Considering different context limitations throughout the process is such a great lesson for me.
