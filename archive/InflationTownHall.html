The Product Request

An Inflation Town Hall Event will be held to increase customer engagement and demonstrate use cases to potential clients.


My Contribution

I proposed the end-to-end customer journey to marketing teams to enhance registration experience. I designed, crafted and built the customer registration emails and in-app notifications using Figma and Pardot.

Success Metrics

Through data analytics, we were informed that the event received 216 registration. Over 70% of participants are external users, some of which are previous customers. The SpendingPulse App also saw a huge usage boost afterwards.

Business Impact

This event demonstrated new use cases of SpendingPulse to power users from Merchant and Financial Institues. After this event, I also shared my experience of using Pardot to design emails to over 5 designers tasked with similar requests in other product teams.

Registration Email

Economists or Financial advisors, who are SpendingPulse customers, will receive an email notifying them of the upcoming Inflation Town Hall Event. They can read through the email to know the time, the topics and the speakders. They can register immediately, share it with colleagues or email SpendingPulse team if they have questions.

In App Modal

For clients in United States, when they first login through SpendingPulse Platform, an in-app modal will automatically open to them. This will be served as a reminder to join and learn more about how to use SpendingPulse to track existing inflation.

In App Banner

For clients who need more time to decide if they want to join the meeting, an in-app banner is displayed on the SpendingPulse Platform. They are only one click away to know more about the event and register before the final date.

Design Process

Step 1: Understand Product Need and Responsibility

After the task was assigned, I listed out our product goal through this project. In order to discuss the responsibility with the marketing team , I crafted a proposed customer journey in order to align both teams to the same page.

Step 2: Led touch points design from email to notifications

With clear responsibilities split between our team and marketing team, I started design and iteterate for three main touch points. I conducted feasibility research through exploring the existing patterns. The concepts were shared with the design team and product team to find the best way to display the information.

Step 3: Built the email through Pardot

Since this was the first event held from our product side, no one in the team know how to use Pardot, an email building tool. I offered to learn the tool and built the email . After two days of exploration, I succesfully built out the emails and shared the update with PMs. As the first one in the design team using Pardot, I have also shared step-by-step process to 5+ designers were tasked with similar projects from other orgs.
