Scheduling

Digitize schedule assignment process for Walmart Transportation Managers (NDA)

Background

Like how collage student register classes, Walmart drivers need to pick schedules every year. These schedules can be regular 5-2, 6-2, 5-5, 6-2-6-3, etc... which defines the number of work day and weekend. 5-2 means work 5 day, then rest 2 days; 6-2-6-3 means work for 6 days, rest for 2 days, then work for 6 days, rest for 3 days. In order to help drivers pick schedules, local transportation office are responsible to inform the drivers when to bid, what schedules they can bid and how to bid. Product team decided to digitize and standardize this process to improve efficacy across the contry in long term.




My role

We went through 1 round of user review, several round of design iteration and 1 round of user testing in this project. Intake

Building users' confidence through Wizard UI

Through research, we discovered that there are several tasks a manager needs to complete before releasing the schedule. Proved by our early concepts, a Wizard UI can help users know clearly where they are at, what to do next and what has been completed. However, in our early concepts, there were some overlapping tasks in the 2nd and 3rd steps, making users confused. We decided to iterate on the process to help users complete one task at one time without looking back. We also discovered that titles and subtexts are necessary to reinforce the goal of each step to reduce errors for a multistep process.




Implementing real data when designing UI

When designing prototypes, designers usually use dummy data and focus on visual elements. However, in this project, I discovered that implementing real data is crucial for pushing design forward. When real data is implemented, we instinctively start to read and try to make sense from the interface. This method pushed us to find a way to display empty data and redesigned the element to help users confirm what they are selecting.
