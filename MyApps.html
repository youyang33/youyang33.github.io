MyApps

One stop shop for product launch at Walmart Supply Chain (NDA)

Background

MyApps Admin Platform is designed for developers at Walmart Supply Chain org to launch, customize and manage code files. Right now, releasing apps and webs to Walmart Distribution Centers can take a long period of time. As 200+ Walmart distribution centers differs in business, faciliaties, staff and policies, developement teams spend duplicated time to customize their code packages on a site-by-site basis. With MyApps Admin portal, developers will manage their code packages in a more efficient and transparent way.




My role

I lead the end-to-end design process for this project, from brainstorming to fixing design details after handoff. I communicated with the development team on a weekly basis, but also flexible for any adhoc discussions. No matter it is a card design or a flow design, I always backup concepts with detailed InVision prototypes for best discussion. I also lead research and bring end users' voice to the team, making sure we are doing the right thing.




Outcome

The reskinned MyApps web has been released to over 200 Distribution Centers. The admin portal design is being developed by the development team. Three files were send out to development team as final deliveries, including an interactive prototype, a design specification and a UI element style guide. The process and outcome was shared within Supply Chain team and my manager commented :"Seriously great job on MyApps - you've pushed it a long way and made a complex process very simple".

Intake

Evaluate product through users' eye

We, product managers or designers, may not know everything. It is important to conduct research to evaluate assumptions. It helps us to understand users' mental model, then how can we serve them better. When designing the flow of editing customize config, we discovered that users feel reluctant to edit on the original version. This makes them feel unsure about the changes. Then I designed a side-by-side view to help them compare and confirm. This design helps us to gain a lot of support when demoing our prototypes to stakeholders.




Design systems are guidelines, not answers

The Walmart design system has been my design guidelines when I worked on this project. Using the design system prevents designers from reinventing the wheel. It fastens my work speed and also guarantees products have the same look and feel.

But it doesn't mean stop innovating. Sometimes the design system doesn't work. When this happened, I framed the question, came up with a proposed solution and discussed it with the design system team. One of my works has pushed the Walmart design system team to release a new site selector component.




Help developers catchup with design details

In an end-to-end design project, it is nothing like hand off and free of troubles. Handoff is like another start for numerous meetings and adhoc questions.

There are several methods I found rather efficient when handoff designs. Encouraging comments in prototypes helps stakeholders to share thoughts without waiting. The style guide helps front-end developers find all instances for a UI element with ease. Backend developers also reference the style guide to check how to reuse UI elements. When sending out design tweaks, I always attach a before/after image to point out the difference. Visual Q&A helps to find visual and interactive gaps between prototype and final product.

All these technics bring the team on the same page. And allows us to find new opportunities even after handoff.




And...

This has been a great experience working directly with developers from brainstorming to development. The design team and tech team have been incredibly supportive. The whole team was always flexible and open-minded. Over-communication is never a problem in this team, more like a habit to pursue something better.
