CinemAll App



I acted as a UX Designer with my classmates Ruturaj, Christa and Danielle for 3 months, collborating on semi-structured interviews and interface design. We finally generated an App prototype to improve the in-theater movie experience for visually impaired moviegoers, who currently needtheater-provided descriptive video devices to enjoy a movie. This project proves a high acceptance in utilizing mobile app to solve the universal issue in scenarios like theater, conferences or even museums, but also undercover some usability problems.






The Challenge
Movie Watch is not a Universal Experience.

We aim at improving the in-theater movie experience for visually impaired moviegoers who currently need theater-provided descriptive video devices to enjoy a movie. 



User Research
Types of Visually Impaired Moviegoers

We surprisely discovered that many movies come with audio description tracks according to the Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 (ADA). Through our pilot research, we identified a neglected, yet attention-needed user segments, the visually impaired movielovers for theaters like AMC, Regal, Cinefest, Landmark, and others. To be more specific, we define our target users range in age from 18 and up, have the normal hearing function, span all genders, have anxieties about going to a movie theater, and may or may not bring a companion with them.

Not Watch, but Enjoy Moives

The visually Impaired may perceive different or less visual elements than others, but the passion for the fatasitic sound, the intriguing story and the engagement with other movie lovers are no difference. Besides, the theaters are renting descriptive video devices for free to them, using audio scripts to retrieve their partically-perceived visual elements.

The Movie Journeys

Through user interviews and participating in the journey, we discovered that users have to be well prepared before getting the theater knowing where have and which associate they should contact to borrow the descriptive headset.







Those "Losing-Users" Moments

1) Before the movie, requiring users to interact with less professional associates, will caused them feeling not valued and not prepared well enough. Also, extra personal information leakage cause some users feeling suspected.

2) In the movie, users have to deal with the headset error by approaching to the front desk,which disrupts users immersive experience and angry for missing important scenes.

Design Direction
Brianstorming Concepts

Concept 1: Smart Kiosks

This solution will aim to cut out interaction with theater associates, who are often times undertrained and cause unnecessary frustrations for visually impaired moviegoers. Additionally, we envision the kiosks to be a universal solution and a one-stop shop for all moviegoers to purchase or pickup tickets and retrieve assistive devices. While we are focused on the visually impaired, this solution could also have a broader adaptation for those who need different types of assistance, such as hearing devices.







Concept 2: In-theater Service App, leveraging existing devices

This solution aims to be low cost, as theaters won’t have to replace their devices. Here, instead we will pair users with devices before they arrive at the theater, therefore alleviating frustrations of interacting with under-trained staff.







Concept 3: In-theater Audio Streaming App, cutting out existing devices

This solution aims to cut down on the learning curve by using the device users already have with them, their smartphone. Users won’t need to lug around additional clunky devices. This solution will also cut out the need to interact with theater associates who often provide incorrectly configured devices for the users.





Design Guidelines and Concept Evaluation

From our research, we discovered that make a low error rate and highly seamless accessible experience is required by the visually impaired. Several design guidelines are generated through affinity diagram to instruct us brainstorming.

  • The visually impaired moviegoers want to be independent and self-prepared before getting to the theater.
  • The intended experience are highly influenced by the theater's technology limitation and staff's awareness.
  • The visually impaired moviegoers can experience more obstacles when being asked to fixed an error.

Concept Statement

Our group decided move forward on the in-theater audio-descrption mobile application. Utilizing the highly advance mobile tech, it has the advantage that users can prepare their audio ahead of time, hugely reduce the least theater-staff interaction and give full privacy to the target users.
The intended app aims to leverage on users' own mobile devices to get access to theaters' audio tracks.

 It should have the benefits to cut down on the learning curve for stumbling on additional devices. It also need to reduce the need to interact with theater associates who often inevitably error.



Design Tradeoff
Copy Right Issue Influences Intended User Flow

We assume that the pupularity of our intended app has great influence with the ability to provide the lateset descriptive audio track. However, the movie producers' right is equally important for a success launch of this product. We have to prevent users take advantage of this app to leak and disseminate new movie content illegally.

Design Iterations
Design for the Visually Impaired

To design for the visually impaired users, none of us have previous experience. We thus referenced some accessible design guidelines to learn how to design for the visually impaired users. We tried to maintain a 5.04 constrast ratio which pass WebAIM standard 4.5:1 as well as maintaining a simple and single column layout; We also maintained a black theme considering the dark showroom environment.



Hi-Fi Prototype
Sign In and Sign Up


Problems

  • 1/6 of users reported that the information in Sign In Page is too much.
Modifications

  • We thus redefined structure to highlight the needed information;

  • Two methods of input are provided in the app: Voice Input and Keyboard Input.

  • Two types of SignUp methods are provided: Social Network Signup or Signup by creating an account.





Find and Select your Movie


Problems

  • 4/6 of users are having trouble with scanning tickets, 2/6 users prefer E-tickets uplooad.

  • 2/6 of users are in need of feedback for a correct scan/upload.
Modifications

  • We embeded both e-ticket upload and ticket scan functions.

  • Feedback for correct scan are provided. We also allow users to make modification based on the detection of the results.

  • Two types of SignUp methods are provided: Social Network Signup or Signup by creating an account.





Problems

  • 1/6 of users prefer manually select tickets, but they cannot know what "manually select" means.

  • 1/6 of users mentioned voice input as a preferred methods to quick sort out theater and movie.
Modifications

  • "Select Manually" is replaced by "Select by Theater and Movie" to indicate the following process.


  • History feature is embeded to reduce users' cognitive and operation load, since based on our interviews, most of the users have one preferred theater.







Sync Audio and Watch Movie


Problems

  • Users' satisfaction rate have the largest standard deviation in the sync process.

  • 2/6 of users have difficulty in understanding why they should connect the WiFi and what does sync means.
Modifications

  • We allow users to choose movie before their arrival to make them feel more independent and well-prepared.

  • Sync is replaced with copy "Request upon arrival", and an indication of how the wifi works are provided when users are outside of wifi scope.

  • Test audio is provided as a step that users could skip, while the sync process is indicated through a blue bar graph.
