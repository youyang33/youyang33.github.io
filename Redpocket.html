Current User Behavior

Through observation, I found there were three interesting social scenarios when teenage users use red pocket. They can send a pocket to boost vibe, to express feelings to a certain person, or as traps for photos.

Gamified Red Pocket Experience
Send Money in Red Pockets

When a teenager want to make fun of their classmates, they can select bullet pocket mode. This allows users to split a small amount of money, sometimes even 50 cents, to several pockets randomly.

Catch Flying Bullet Red Pockets

When a bullet pocket is sent, users in this chat can race for the most amount of money by catching the flying bullet pockets. This uncertainty, boost by bomb pockets, can greatly engage users in a group chat.

The Competition is On!

After all the pockets are sent out, teenagers can reivew the result. This gives them new social topics about who participated, who get the least amount of money and who get the most amount of money. As this is a random game, teenagers can always laugh at the ones who lag behind without hurting each others‚Äô feeling.

How did I find new design directions when users don‚Äôt know what they want?

Step 1

My mentor encouraged me to create some new ideas for our users. I decided to start from understanding user motivations. Through several rounds of networking, I managed to find a teenager who was willing to add me to their group chats. This allowed me to observe teenager social behaviors and uncover their intentions when using red pocket service in a group chat.

Step 2

I brainstormed several different concepts to help teenagers boost vibes, share photos or express feelings. Some concepts include gesture red pockets, birthday red pockets, photo red pockets. The bullet red pocket was reported to be the most interesting, the most flexible, and be suitable for all three scenarios.

Step 3

Later, I tested concepts with teenagers and identified gaps between what is designed and users interpretation. 3 out of 5 end users mentioned this service is fun and can be used to make fun of their friends in a group chat to boost vibes. Some modifications were suggested to simply the service and clear confusions.

Step 4

I created a functional prototype using Framer and invited product managers to use. The PM was concerned about the user pool size, but was very attracted to the solution. Several month later, my mentor told me that it was selected as one of the 2019 Spring Festival Marketing Campaign.
