CinemAll App

The Challenge

In-theater movie experience for the visually impaired moviegoers is not as ideal as the one for the normal vision moviegoers.

To enjoy a movie, the visually impaired moviegoers currently need to rent theater-provided descriptive video devices , which often have technical problems that disrupt an immersive movie watching experience.

Problem Identified

Against their wish of being independent, the renting process requires the visually impaired rely on theater associates inevitably.

Before the movie, the visually impaired moviegoers have to arrive earlier than others with normal vision, line in front of service desk, request the DVS headsets from certain associates, and then leave their personal information as an exchange. There are the extra time and effort the visually impaired need to spend to find the right associates, most of whom usually struggle to set the right DVS streaming channel. Complaint by the visually impaired, this renting process leaves an impression to them that they need a special care and are being suspected.

Limited by hardware technology, the malfunction of the DVS headsets often disrupt the watching experience, which is supposed to be immersive.

Unstable streaming signal and malfunction of the DVS headsets usually happen when users are enjoying their movie. The theater lack a way to prevent that and have to require the visually impaired to go back to the service desk and change to another one. When the users come back, they often miss a big portion of the movie, which is claimed as frustrating.

Our Solution

Rather than utilizing the in-theater DVS headsets, by leveraging on users’ own mobile phone, we should be able to improve the immersive movie watching experience in reducing customer-staff interaction and the possibility of device malfunction.

We hypothesis that this mobile app will help to improve the theater ticket sales by encouraging more visually impaired moviegoers to the theaters, and thus build a stronger brand loyalty for theater companies like Regal, Landmark and so on.

Design Process

We focus on creating highly intuitive, usable and impactful digital products and services. Ideas is seamlessly integrated into our design process to produce the most effective, elegant and engaging work.

None of our group member have experience designing for the visually impaired, we learned from prior research that the Visually Impaired users leverage on Voice Over Functions to use their mobile app. Comparing different prototype tools, including Axure, Framer, Invision and JustInMind, we discovered that JustInMind has the function to include audio files to the prototype.

03. User Testing

We tried to scedule meetings with the Center for the Visually Imparied, however due to time confliction, the participants couldn't made it to our testing sessions. Thus, we borrowed the Visually Impaired Glasses. I was mainly resonsiple for taking notes, observing participants' behavior and then lead the qualitative data analysis. I visualized all the qualtivative feedback generated from think-aloud protocol on our flow chart. This techniques enabled the group to quickly overview users'feedback as well as communicate with other designers in our class.

05 Reflection

Considering the context when designing tasks. This App serves for a whole process from purchasing movie tickets to walk out the theater. Different contexts have high influence on how each stakeholder behave. For example, the manager at the Regal Cinema told us, they wouldn't provide service prior the arrival of the users. However, the visually impaired users would love to be prepared before their arrival. Considering different context limitations throughout the process is such a great lesson for me.
