Reusable Research
ON-GOING, EXPECTED TO FINISH IN APRIL,2018

Required by management team, UXers in The Home Depot (THD) need to do in-field user research at least twice in a week. They will write summary post on a inner wordpress powered research sharing platform, which this project mainly focus on improving.

Business Goal

Inspired by WeWork’s Tag Feature, the Home Depot starts to rebuild its Research Repository for better user experience to promote in-company discussion.

The research repository should be able to search for what prior research have been done to avoid money waste and to bring end users voice to the business stakeholders to make them trust in the Home Depot’s design decisions.

Stakeholders and Target Users

The main stakeholders that influenced by this Reusable Research Project include UXers of THD, Engineer Team of THD and Investors of THD and Business Partners. The primary users of the Research Repository Platform are the UXers of THD.

Problem Identified

Most of the users often use the research repository, mainly for writing research post and read research posts and most of them are somewhat unsatisfied. 52.50% of the users find searching posts the most difficult to conduct on the platform. The sharing acitivity is viewed as not hard but it is the least frequent activity, the frequency of which the management team aim to improve.

Research Methods

1.1 Marketing and Competitor Analysis

To understand the trend as well as the industry standard, one market research, literature review and one pilot user interview was conducted. Through these research, I discover that the Home Depot is leading in its awareness of facilitating inner research sharing and communicating.

1.3 Semi-Structured Interviews and Focus Group Discussion

Four 60 minutes interviews were conducted with 4 Uxers in the HD. Questions like “How long have you been in the Home Depot? What is your experience in using the repository?” were asked. Also, One focus group discussion was conducted in the Home Depot with 5 participants in different Orange Team. Questions like “ What are the possibilities of the repository?” “ What is your least like experience in the repository is discussed.” The qualitative data collected through the process were analyzed through journey map.

This process uncovered three scenarios that UXers are having less pleasant experience. Users' vacabulary are also collected as answers for the survey.

1.4 Survey

A questionnaire is released and received 49 responses in THD's Slack Channel, questions includes their role in the HD, their experience in terms of the four identified scenarios, their preference towards new design solutions are asked. It is expected to gather data before the end of this semester by prioritizing the problems identified and explore users’ actual behaviors.

1.5 Survey Result Analysis

As discussed above, 52.50% of the users find searching posts the most difficult to conduct on the platform. The sharing acitivity is viewed as not hard but it is the least frequent activity, the frequency of which the management team aim to improve.

Users search for contents mainly to learn from others'research strategy, to know certain research result as well as to avoid duplicate research. Currently, the most popular search method inlcude the search box and the front page navigation. Users thinks that a lack of advanced filtering mechanism and a lack of knowledge on professional terms and jargons are the main reasons that accounts for the difficulty to search.

Content creators and content consumers, they are all paying most of their attention to high level research summaries and findings. But UXers as content creaters also pay attention to the storytelling methods while content consumers are paying attention to find posts' relevance to what they are actually looking for,which they find the most difficulty..

Within-company sharing acitivity is the lowest frequent activity among all sharing types, which the management team aimed at improving. Not knowing who might be interested in their research post is the main reason that account for the low frequency rate.

1.5 Updated User Journey

A questionnaire is released and received 49 responses in THD's Slack Channel, questions includes their role in the HD, their experience in terms of the four identified scenarios, their preference towards new design solutions are asked. It is expected to gather data before the end of this semester by prioritizing the problems identified and explore users’ actual behaviors.

1.6 Key Insights

1. Content Consumers are having difficulty finding related research methods and results while content creaters don't know who are interested in their posts.

2. Content creaters and content consumers all care about high level research summaries, but content creators are having difficulty find time to write and writing in a easy-understanding way.

3. Professional terms/jargons are great barriers to content readers to find and understand prior research.

4. Content consumers and creators hope to dicuss on the research rather than merely share posts.
