Gamified Payment Design

Business Goal

Sending red envelope to relatives and friends through social media as a way to send best wishes has been a popular phenomenon in China. User experience design has been crucial to increase daily and monthly active users. This project was to research teenager payment behaviors, discover new teenager payment scenarios as well as prepare for the upcoming red envelope war among tech giants.

Target Users

QQ, a sister product to Wechat, is the most popular social media platform for young people, generation after generation. It attracts 868 million monthly active users and 652 million active mobile users, the majority (59.6%) of which are aged between 10 to 29, coming from Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities.

The Outcome

The gamified QQ Red Envelope concept is selected to launch and under development.

Through user research, wireframing and mid-fidelity demo presentation, I won support from my design team and obtained approval from my design leader to push the concept to the product team. After several rounds of high fidelity prototype iterations with my advisor Freywang and usability testing with 7 users, the concept as well as the user feedback was selected by the Product Manager and entered the development cycle.

Problems I Addressed Through This Project

1. To better understand the users.

How is young people's payment behaivor different from adults'?
What are the main social payment scenarios for the users?
What are the reasons that teenagers use QQ Red Envelope in group chatting?

2. To duplicate success through user research methods.

How to get user feedback through interviews in limited project time period?
How to extract reliable user scenarios from user interviews?
What are the available metrics to measure user acceptance level to new product?
