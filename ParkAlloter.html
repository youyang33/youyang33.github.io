Park Alloter App

Role UX Designer
Team Reza, Mianzhen, Seunghyun, Duan
Type School Group Project

This projects aims at utilizing mobile app technology to help drivers make a wise and satisfying parking decision with the user updatable parking database. I worked as a UX Designer conducting 6 semi-structured interviews, generated the user interface and conducted user testing with four software engineers.

THE CHALLENGE

Find An Ideal Parking Efficiently

Our group aims at creating a mobile application to help drivers make a satisfying parking decision with the user updatable parking database. Through our user research, we identified that a delay of knowing information like parking fee and parking capacity is our main user pain point.

USER RESEARCH

Drivers Leverage on Episodic Memory to Park

I interviewed 6 drivers to understand what their current experience was. Based on their description, we discovered that drivers heavily rely on assumptions driven by their prior experience to judge where to park.


Drivers Find Parking Behind the Wheel

Based on interview results, I draw a userstory board to share with four software engineers to pitch the identified user behaviors. To our suprise, most of the users don't search for parking ahead of time. Rather, they find parking while they were behind the wheel.

Use Case Identification

1. Spotify an interesting shop and want to find an available street parking spot.

  • Open the app;
  • Search nearby parking;
  • Filter for street parking;
  • Filter for availability parking;
  • View parking opitons;
  • Compare distance from the parking lot to the shop;
  • Choose a street parking;
  • Navigate

2. The user wants to visit Piedmont Park and would like a spot near the Park Fountain.

  • Open the app;
  • Search for Piedmont Park
  • View Piedmont Park Recommended Spots;
  • Use the map to estimate the nearest park spot near Fountain
  • Save
  • Navigate

3. The user wants to park near Fox Theater for a show that will starts in 30 minutes.

  • Open the app;
  • Search for Fox Theater
  • Search for parking nearest to Fox Theater;
  • Calculate the time needed from navigating to walking back to the theater
  • Navigate

Our Solutions

Meet Users' Expectations Tightly On the First Page

We assumed that how tightly the first page meet new-registered users' expectation will have a high influence on users' retention rate. New registered users are resources to be transfered into seed users and can be the activators of the snowball effect. We aim to communicate our service and values through right amount of information to avoid disppointing feeling that seed users can feel if they cannot find values quickly in our app.



Parking Recommentations Anchored by Destination

Parking is not users' primary goal, rather it is an inevitable step in approaching to their destinations. Allowing users to view and decide their parking alternatives anchored by the intended destination will create an indication that Park Alloter is professionally help them approaching their goals efficently. Such design will create a trustworthy feeling from our users towards our service.



Points Based Membership

To encourage user reports quantity, we provide a points based memebership plan to encourage users contribution. Parking discounts will be provided both as advertisments for parking lots and rewards to users as a marketing appeal.

To ensure high quality of user reports, recommendation and capacity info retrieved will be updated every 15mins from backend, which thereby requires the user reports to be finished in 15 minutes after they arrived.



An Efficent Journey End: Find Parking Entrance

To better help users finish their parking, an important but not urgent feature: parking entrance image is introduced to increase app competitiveness among all the navigation and parking apps. Upon arrival, the entrance image that users updated will show on the screen to guide users until the last minutes.

Reflections

What I learned from the Projects

Respect Timeline but Strive for the best user experience. When working with software engineers, it was a hard experience to pursuade them avoiding providing solutions immediately. User stories, interview quotes are great tools to talk with engineer team and let them understand the value of user experience. Also, many commnication like what are feasible and what are not should be declaired at first. We cut down a lot of features due to the development load and I realized that an estimation of time needed for each feature could better solve such problems

Users sometimes compromise accuracy versues time needed. Trough quick validation and usability testing, we discovered that if there is a way like using memory to find the parking spots, users won't take time to search for someting more accurate. This was a huge challenge that we learned and narrowed down our design scope as well as senarios. This let me aware that we should talk in more depth with the users rather than assume that they would do something as it is accurate.
