Project Summary

Researched and designed a comparison view for Destination App to drive engagement and renewals.


My Contribution

I designed the end-to-end flow for users to enable this business use cases. 3 concepts were presented to clients to validate their needs and wants. When facing huge pushbacks from development team, I actively led the discussion to discuss pros and cons.

Success Metrics

After launch, we will use Splunk to track customer engagement by active users and their log in activities. We will also track client feedback with Sales and Delivery team.

Business Impact

This feature is one of the largest feature launches in Destination Platform. It will allow our targer users, Destination Marketing Officers to do comprehensive analysis in this platform. It is a key strategic business step to obtain more platform renewal with DMO departments.

Compare Mode Entry Button

Destination Platform is targeted at Destination Management officers who analyze visitor volume and spend data to drive traffic to their areas. We provides them a data platform powered by Mastercard payment data.

Previously, users have to export by origins and destinations if they need to compare different sectors and visitor source. To make it easier for users, a compare button is introduced to draw attention.

Compare Page

In the page, users have the capability to add lines, delete lines or edit lines to compare KPIs among different destination, origin and sectors.

Edge Case: 10 Lines Added.

When 10 lines are added to the page, the add button will be disabled. Users have the capability to hover over a line or edit a line.

Process Highlights

Validate to understand the needs

We heard from sales and delivery team that "Clients want to compare". After product discussion and design discussion, we find it could mean several different directions. In order to figure out the exact needs, I created three different concepts for PM to validate with our clients in a Mastercard event hosted in Europe in July 2023. After the meeting, PM brought us good news that we can focus on one of the direction.

Concept reviews with Product and Tech

We went through two rounds of tech review to understand what is feasible and what is not. In the meeting, we confirmed a few new points to improve and change based on tech capability and user habits.

Finalize design specifications

After the concept was finalized, product team and development team went on a strong debate on whether to use this design or reuse what we had been doing before. In order to help buy in, I helped the team to mockup all possible directions based on pushbacks. This helps the team to view the pros and cons visually.

With limited timeline, it has been a huge challenge to get buy-in from development team. Even though business side is very excited about this new design, dev team conducted two rounds of review questioning if the new design is necessary. I compared 5 different concepts using Figma discussing back and forth for potential ideas. Luckily, we decided to keep the core design and change one of the button placement.
