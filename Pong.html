Pong Game

As technology makes it possible for people to live and work farther away from each other, it becomes harder to interact directly with friends and family in the same place. It is not uncommon to have colleagues that you know only through email, family that you only talk to through skype or facetime, and best friends that you only see as avatars on your television screen. These interactions have many benefits, but the increase in sedentary screen time is having an adverse effect on public health . Something must be done to enable people to physically interact with their communities, even if those communities are only accessible through the internet

Scenario Write Up

Two friends decide that they want to hang out. The first (Robert) is at home, and the second (Rachel) is at a park. Each of them has a mobile device. Robert has his iPad tablet, and Rachel has her iphone. Robert uses his tablet to send a message to meet. Then he holds his tablet up to the fiducial marker he has hanging on his wall. Rachel goes to a fiducial marker located at the park and does the same thing. As soon as she does, Robert and Rachel can each see each other in their own spaces as avatars from within the augmented reality frame of their devices. Each of their audio streams is localized so as to sound as if it is coming directly from the avatar.

After talking for a few minutes, they decide that they want to play a game. Rachel selects “pong”, and Robert approves. Both Robert’s room and an area within Rachel’s park are augmented with digital barriers and a game board. Rachel places her phone into a headset to interact with the world in three dimensions, whereas Robert plays from his tablet in 2D. The both run from one corner of the augmented space to the other in order to block the pong ball with their bodies. The ball gets faster and faster with each successful hit, until one of the two players cannot keep up.
