Gamified Payment Design

Business Goal

Red Pocket is the main payment service for QQ users, supporting game purchase, social payment behaviors and other third party shopping behaviors on QQ platform. I led this project as a product designer to active daily usage of QQ Red Pocket, one of the main features of QQ Wallet.

Target Users

QQ is the second largest social media in China and also the most popular social media for generation Z. The users are mainly students from primary school to college. They use QQ for its dynamic functions.

Problems Identified

Teenagers have limited money, but they pay for fun.

The Results

Through through ux walkthrough, I delivered a UX breakdown reports to the team to communicate the priority. After discussion, I updated the prior QQ Backend Platform styleguide before new services launch.

Design Process

The project lasts 2 mounths with back-and-force desicion makings from the management teams, the product teams, design teams and development teams. I collaborated with a Senior Product Manager, communciated key user insights and delivered ux specifications to him.

01 Identify Key UX Breakdowns

Reported by the product managers, users are complainting about inconsistancy over the platform. Meanwhile my team were preparing for new services the QQ Wallet | Business Official Account. The product manager decided to hand this work to me and improve user stastisfaction.

Method: EaseNet ToB Platform Quick Checklist

To handle those negative comments in our platform, I set up a meeting with UX Design Lead, Design Director and Product Manager teams to discuss our business goal, timeline and human resources. Through this meeting, I decided to conduct a cognitive walkthrough to locate the key breakdowns in key flows.


Discovery: Abused Table Usage that Lacked Consistency

Among all the 14 pages that I identified as having usability issue, 9 pages has issue with its tables components. While tables are efficient in quick scanning, yet the current layout lacked a consistent regualtion in its presentation way.

Priority and Work Plan: Improve Table Usability Problems First

Communicating with the product managers, I identified that the development resources we could rely on is very limited. Only one UI engineer is available working on this project while he is filled with deadlines with higher priority work. How to reduce his work load to unsure a practicle outcome is my main problem.

02 Design and Iterations

Method: Exhaustive Method and Category

I listed the interfaces where invoved table modules and compared them with the previous styleguide. As I found out, the previous styleguide supports well for the reading model, however, with new features involved, the user activitis cannot be well supported. Each previous designers design on their own way which caused the inconsistency across platforms. Thus I categorized the components, interactions and scenarios existed across platforms, which works as a clue to improve the table consistency.

Layout Design: Components and Group

Inferred by walkthrough and category, I decided to improve platform consistency from basic elements. When designing, I follow the rule that each category should follow the same standard to avoid cognitive confusion.

03 Guidance

Guide the Design of QQ Light Application Backend Platform

After I updated the styleguide for tables, forms, posts, I started working on the QQ Light Application Backend Platform. Using the updated styleguide as reference, the design follows the same visual design with improved usability. Such practice saves time and make iterations on the future platforms.

04 Reflections

Styleguide is a way to communicate keypoints with Design and Development Teams

My final deliveries are update of the system styleguide. Through this work, our team also achived an agreement that, while styleguide is often treated as a library for product UI, it is also a way to communicate key user behaviors with product managers. Select the most typical user behavior as the key module in styleguide can make sure these key actions will be proporly developed.
